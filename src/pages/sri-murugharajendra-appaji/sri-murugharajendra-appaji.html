Child Murugharajendra is born... Born to smt. Sunandamma and Channaveerayya, on Dec 2, 1985 at Jambaga village, Aland Tq, Kalaburgi district. he was named Murugharajendra.

Early Childhood

Even as a child Murugharajendra was ardently pious. at school he was an obedient and conscientious student. several where the instances of his boyhood days when he displayed exceptional qualities of wisdom and farsightedness. from a very young age, he began expressing to his friends a desire to renounce the world and use to utter the words " I am supreme, born to lead you all ", and has shown great leadership qualities at the young age and he has a great soft corner for Sadhu's and underprivileged people. "At the tender years of three and four, whenever a beggar appeared at the door and raised his cry, Sathya left his play and rushed inside to hand out grain or food."

MurugharajendraYoung Appaji would keep sitting in deep meditation for long hours, at very young age and doing so with his friends one day a snake appears and all his friends ran to their feet by seeing Cobra, but since he was in deep meditation, only neighbours and his parents woke him up from the meditation, by seeing this many believed that he is supreme.

Murugharajendra realized that god alone is one reality and essence of life, he saw that school education could be used merely for the purpose of earning bread and fulfilling worldly enjoyment, he become disenchanted to education and discontinued studies, came back to his village.

Chosen Successor(Uttaradhikari)

In DikshaSri sri Shadakshari Shivayogi Siddarameshwara appaji decided to choose the Murugharajendra as his successor. necessary arrangements were made on 12 march 2000 on the auspicious day of Ramnavmi and mugalkhoda Jatre named him as successor to mugalkhod, Jidga and all other 365 Mutts spread across India.

Now the Murugharajendra has turned into Balayogi Murugharajendra even before he had attained the age of 15.

Sri sri Balayogi Murugharajendra under took extensive tours, visiting all mutts, across India. where ever he went large number of devotees received his blessings in great joy.

On Feb 2004 sri sri Shadakshari Shivayogi Siddharameshwara appaji ended his mortal life at Jidga, about few days later Sri Sri Murugharajendra appaji took over as Mathadhipathi of mugalkhod, Jidga and all other 365 mutts spread across India.

Able Administrator

Many were the tasks that lay ahead of new Jagadguru, he started improving the affairs of the mutts, and built Sri Siddarameshwara Bhakta Nivas at Jidga.

Pujya appaji's sympathy for the poor and the lower classes of society is too well known to be mentioned here.

Nittya Annadaana (Dasoha):

The old tradition of generous hospitality to all those who enter the portals of mutt. Every one of the thousands of pilgrimage who daily visit these mutts is an honoured guest irrespective of cast, creed, culture or status. As always Free food and shelter is provided to pilgrims.

Darshana and Counseling:

Devotees who come to mutt with their woes and problems are given a patient hearing and guidance by pujya Appaji. The mutt practices a unique way to treat devotees by giving Prasda pudis to every individual who comes to darshana.

Samuvik Vivaha (Mass Marriages):

In an endeavour to eradicate social evils like dowry, casteism, untouchability and financial debt, Pujya Appaji introduced Samuvik vivaha. The expenses of the wedding dress, mangala sutra and wedding feast for limited number of couples guest are borne by the mutt.

Free Accommodation and Dasoha for School Children:

In an effort to remove child labouring and illiteracy for needy children mutt provides free Food, accommodation, dress material and stationary (like books, Note books, pen etc.).

Siddashri Adamya Chetana Prashasti:

Pujya appaji gives out a Siddhashri Adamya Chetana Prashasthi on every Guruvandana programe of Sri Sri Shadakshari Shivayogi Siddarameshwar Appaji, this award is a cheque of rupees 1 lakh and a certificate carrying Siddashri Prashasthi.

Siddashri Prashasthi is given to an individual who has done remarkable or outstanding work in discharging his/her duties or services to the mankind or improving lives of people.

This shows the Appaji's commitment to encourage and motivate the individuals, who practiced unselfishness in discharging their duties to serve the society.

Prasadam, blessed by the Pujya Appaji are distributed to the patients in hospitals, inmates of jails, orphanages, etc.

Victims of flood, famine, earthquake and other natural calamities are being helped in various ways under the orders of the Pujya Appaji.
